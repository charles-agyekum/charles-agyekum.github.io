How every piece is built

The same discipline, every time.

Clean first, and prove it

Audit the raw data before any chart. On one build a UNIQUE-based check caught seven data-quality issues — a quantity column spelling “One” as text, a hidden trailing space breaking a slicer — before a single visual was drawn.

Verify the headline figure

Every number that gets quoted is reconciled to source first. If a dashboard says 54.8% of orders run late, that figure has been traced back through 180,519 rows before it is allowed to ship.

End on a recommendation

Finish with a decision a manager can act on — not a description of the chart. Re-baseline the lead time. Cap the discount on Tables. Target retention at the segments that actually churn.

Power BI · Flagship

DataCo Smart Supply Chain — Late Delivery & OTIF

180,519 supply-chain order lines. Over half of all orders broke their delivery promise — flat near 55% for three years, so the problem is structural, not a slip. The lever is shipping mode: First Class runs late 95% of the time against Standard’s 38%, sold as one-day but reliably taking two. Re-baseline the lead time and de-risk the $2.14M of profit tied to late orders.

Power BI · £12.6M · 11.6% margin

Superstore — Order Fulfilment & Profitability

51,290 order lines through Power Query ETL, a star-schema model and seven DAX measures. Sales healthy at £12.6M but margin thin at 11.6%. The leak traced to one sub-category — Tables — losing £64,083 at a 29% average discount. Cap the discount there; leave fulfilment alone.
